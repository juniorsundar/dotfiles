# Pi-native research orchestrator

We will build deep research as a pi-native Research Orchestrator rather than faithfully emulating Alibaba DeepResearch's literal `<Action>` / `<observation>` protocol or wrapping subagents in v1. The orchestrator owns Research Proposals, user approval, workspace-local artifacts, budgets, progress digests, readiness checks, and human-readable views, while Tongyi DeepResearch acts as the single Research Brain for query planning, source prioritization, orchestrator-invoked Source Note extraction, synthesis, and early-stop recommendations. This preserves pi visualization and control, handles Tongyi's small practical context through file-backed run artifacts and summaries, and leaves subagent integration and richer provider/document-parser support as later extensions.

Clarification: in v1, the Research Brain does not receive direct tool access and is not a tool-using agent. It proposes a limited set of structured research intents, while the Research Orchestrator validates those intents and exclusively performs side-effecting operations such as search, fetch, local reads, artifact writes, budget accounting, progress reporting, and citation validation. This keeps budgets, approvals, persistence, and auditability under pi-native control instead of recreating Alibaba's literal action protocol inside the model.

V1 also limits execution to one active Research Run per Pi session/workspace to keep lifecycle, progress reporting, shutdown handling, and budget accounting predictable. This is a scope constraint, not a fundamental property of the Research Orchestrator model; future versions may add parallel runs without changing the Brain-vs-Orchestrator boundary.
